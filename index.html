Ph.D. Research & Projects

A New Framework for Brain Tumor Detection and Classification in MRI Images

Brain tumor detection in MRI images presents significant challenges due to the complexity and variability of tumor manifestations. This research proposes an integrated framework using the proposed MCLAHE (Modified Contrast-Limited Adaptive Histogram Equalization) and proposed MPCA (Modified Principal Component Analysis) to form a superior tumor segmentation and classification pipeline.

Evaluated on multiple datasets — Web Images, BRATS-2015, and Live clinical images — demonstrating significantly superior tumor localization and classification accuracy even in low-contrast MRI brain images. This computer-assisted diagnostic tool automates detection, localization, segmentation, and classification of brain tumor areas, reducing dependency on subjective radiologist assessments.
